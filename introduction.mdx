## The p-hacking problem

Clinical research has a reproducibility crisis. A researcher can unconsciously pick the model specification that gives a significant p-value — choosing which covariates to include, which model family to use, or how to handle outliers in ways that inflate apparent significance without realising it. This is p-hacking, and it's endemic.

The result: findings that look solid in one lab fail to replicate in the next. Journals reject papers for lack of robustness checks. FDA reviewers scrutinise trial analyses. Co-authors disagree about which covariates belong in the model.

**Omniverse makes that impossible to hide.** Instead of fitting one regression and hoping it's the right choice, Omniverse fits every defensible specification simultaneously — different covariate subsets, model families, and automatic robust alternatives when statistical assumptions are violated. You see the full picture.

## What Omniverse does

Upload a CSV. Describe your hypothesis in plain English. Omniverse:

1. Profiles every column, flags distribution issues, and uses AI to walk you through variable definitions
2. Extracts your outcome, predictors, and confounders from the conversation — before you see any results
3. Runs every valid model specification (typically 350–400 regression specs for a standard dataset)
4. Applies Benjamini-Hochberg FDR correction across **all** specifications simultaneously
5. Presents the robustness percentage, specification curve, covariate DAGs, and classifier benchmarks

If your effect holds across 80%+ of specifications after FDR correction, it's a strong finding. Below 50%, treat it with caution.

## Who it's for

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Clinical researchers" icon="microscope">
    You want findings that hold up. Omniverse lets you demonstrate robustness before submission — not just report the one specification that worked. Run the multiverse, attach the robustness % to your paper, and let reviewers see the full specification curve.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Biostatisticians" icon="chart-bar">
    You already know about multiverse analysis but writing the loop yourself — handling assumption violations, applying FDR correction across all specs, generating interpretable visualisations — takes hours. Omniverse does it in under five minutes from a CSV and plain-English intent.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Journal reviewers and editors" icon="book-open">
    You're seeing more papers claim robustness without showing it. Ask authors to run Omniverse and attach the specification curve. A tight band of consistent effects means the finding is real. A scattered mess means it depends on arbitrary modeling choices.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="FDA and regulatory reviewers" icon="shield-halved">
    Trial analysis plans are locked in advance, but post-hoc sensitivity analyses still require scrutiny. Omniverse's full specification table — every model, every covariate subset, every corrected p-value — gives you the complete audit trail.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## How AI assists the workflow

Claude (claude-sonnet-4-6 via Anthropic API) is active at three points in the workflow:

- **Data profiling chat** — Claude reads your column profiles, flags skewed distributions and encoding issues, and suggests variable roles based on your data.
- **Study intent chat** — You describe your hypothesis in plain English; Claude extracts the outcome variable, predictors, and confounders as a structured object that drives the analysis.
- **Results chat** — After analysis completes, ask plain-English questions about your findings. Claude has access to the full specification table, robustness %, and summary statistics.

<Note>
  Omniverse is a proof-of-concept built at the YCombinator Bio x AI Hackathon. It is not production-ready: there is no authentication, no persistent storage, and no HIPAA-compliant deployment. All session data is held in memory and lost when the server restarts. See the [quickstart](/quickstart) for setup details.
</Note>
