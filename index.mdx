Omniverse is an AI-powered clinical research analysis platform that makes your findings reproducible. Instead of fitting one regression and hoping it's the right one, Omniverse runs every defensible model specification — different covariate combinations, model families, and transformations — then tells you what percentage of specifications agree with each other.

If your effect holds across 90% of specifications, it's robust. If it only appears in 10%, it's likely an artifact of your modeling choices.

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Upload your dataset">
    Upload a CSV file. Omniverse profiles every column — detecting distributions, skewness, missing data, and potential encoding issues — then an AI assistant walks you through variable definitions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Define your study intent">
    Describe your hypothesis in plain English. Omniverse extracts the outcome variable, predictors, and confounders in a structured format before you see any results — eliminating post-hoc specification selection.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run the multiverse analysis">
    Omniverse runs every valid model specification: different covariate subsets, model families (OLS, penalized regression, logistic, quantile), and automatic robust alternatives when assumptions are violated.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Interpret your results">
    Review the robustness percentage, specification curve, and per-specification DAGs. Ask the AI assistant plain-English questions about your findings. Export publication-ready plots.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Why multiverse analysis?

P-hacking is endemic in clinical research. A researcher can unconsciously pick the model specification that gives them a significant p-value — choosing which covariates to include, which model family to use, or how to handle outliers in ways that inflate apparent significance.

Omniverse makes that impossible to hide. By running all valid specifications simultaneously and applying Benjamini-Hochberg FDR correction across the entire family of tests, you get an honest picture of how robust your finding actually is.
